# End-of-round beta feedback email (draft)

The retired in-app survey, repurposed to send manually to testers at the end
of this beta round. Reply-by-email keeps it zero-friction — no forms, no
sign-in. Trim questions freely; shorter gets more replies.

---

**Subject:** SquirreLingo beta — 5 minutes of your honest opinions? 🐿️

Hi {name},

This round of beta testing is wrapping up, and your take on it would genuinely shape what gets built next. Just hit reply and answer inline — skip anything you like, half-sentences welcome, and the critical stuff is the most useful stuff.

**The basics**
1. What device and browser did you mainly use?
2. Roughly how many practice sessions did you complete? (1–2 / 3–5 / 6–10 / more)

**First impressions**
3. Did anything confuse you or slow you down when you first opened the app?
4. Did you understand how rounds and scoring worked within your first session?

**The core experience**
5. Which categories did you use (vocabulary / grammar / idioms / phonetics), and which was your favorite — why?
6. How did the difficulty feel — too easy, just right, too hard?
7. How did the session length feel — too short, just right, too long?
8. Any thoughts on the progress/mastery tracking?

**Bugs & performance**
9. Did you hit any bugs or glitches? What happened? (If you saw an error code like SQ-XXXXXX, include it!)
10. Did anything feel slow, or look wrong on your screen?

**The big picture**
11. What's the ONE thing you'd fix or change first?
12. What feature would make you use SquirreLingo every day?
13. If SquirreLingo disappeared tomorrow, how would you feel — and would you use something else instead?
14. On a 0–10 scale, how likely are you to recommend it to a friend learning a language?
15. Anything else — praise, complaints, wild ideas?

Thank you for testing — every reply genuinely gets read and acted on. The bug you report today is the fix in next week's changelog.

— {your name}
SquirreLingo
